Plurals of letters and numbers (for readability)

Contracted forms
  • I’m [am]
  • they’re [are]

Comma ,
Separating main clauses (with different subjects)
  • It was cold outside, but we decided to go out for a walk anyway.
Separating subordinate clauses from main clauses
  • If you have any problems, just call me. [But: Just call me if you have any problems.]
  • We should be able to finish the work by the end of the week, if nothing unexpected turns up between now and then. [the clauses are particularly long]
Separating non-defining relative clauses from main clauses
  • My next-door neighbour, who works from home, is keeping an eye on the house while we’re away.
  • She moved to Los Angeles, where she was immediately signed as a singer songwriter.
Separating items in a list
  • She got out bread, butter, and jam.
  • They breed dogs, cats, rabbits and hamsters.
Separating adjectives
  • It was a hot, dry and dusty road.
  • It’s wet, cold and windy outside.
With adverbials
  • However, police would not confirm this rumour.
  • Therefore, I try to avoid using the car as much as possible.
With question tags and short responses
  • It’s quite cold today, isn’t it?
  • Are you the mother of these children? – Yes, I am.
With vocatives
  • And now, ladies and gentlemen, please raise your glasses in a toast to the happy couple.
  • Come on, Olivia, be reasonable.
With discourse markers
  • Well, believe it or not, I actually passed!
  • Actually, I quite enjoyed it.
In reported speech (if there is no ? or ! after the quotation)
  • ‘I don’t understand this question,’ said Peter.
  • Peter said, ‘I don’t understand this question.’
In dates (between the day and the year)
  • March 31, 2011

Direct speech
  • ‘Have you been to the new shopping precinct yet?’ enquired Shona.
  • “I’ve already seen it,” John replied.
  • ‘There is’, Monica said, ‘nothing we can do about it.’
Drawing attention to a word
  • The word ‘book’ can be used as a noun or a verb.
Indicating an unusual use of a word
  • She pointed out that websites used for internet voting could be ‘spoofed’.
Distancing oneself from a word
  • I don’t agree with this ‘mercy killing’ business.
